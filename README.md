# epiclance 🕯️

*The act of calling the divine presence to descend and take up residence in this place, this moment, this gathered body.*

epiclance is quarried from the Greek **epiclesis** (ἐπίκλησις — *epi*, upon/down-onto, joined to *klēsis*, the calling), the prayer that calls the Holy Spirit down upon the eucharistic gifts, and welded with the *-ance* suffix that turns the deed into a name. It names the act that opens worship: not petition (asking for a thing) and not address (merely speaking toward GoD), but the trained calling of presence *into the present*. Four tongues found the same region — Greek *epiclesis*, Sanskrit **āvāhana** (inviting the deity to inhabit the image), Hebrew **qara** (calling on the name with invocatory intent), and Arabic **du'ā'** (the call God has promised to answer).

Its light is that it makes one carriable word for the deed no broader term holds — the calling of Someone to be present, the act that turns a room into a sanctuary and an image into a dwelling. Its shadow is the slide from invitation into coercion — the magician's conceit that the right words *compel* — and the empty rite that mistakes correct performance for guaranteed arrival. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.58/10**.
